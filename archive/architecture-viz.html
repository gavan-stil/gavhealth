Dashboard Page
Your daily health snapshot. Loads when you open the app. Pull down to refresh everything at once. Each card loads its data independently so a slow card doesn't block the rest.
Components (the visible cards)
GoalRingsRow
Three circular rings at the top showing daily progress: Sleep score, Step count, and Protein intake — like Apple's activity rings but for your health goals.
ReadinessCard
The big score (0–100) telling you how recovered your body is today. It's computed from sleep quality, resting heart rate trend, and recent training load. The narrative below the score is written by the backend.
QuickStatsRow
A compact single row of today's key numbers: mood, energy, water consumed, calories, and protein — updated in real time as you log.
ActivityChart
A bar chart of your activities over the last 14 days — runs, workouts, sauna sessions. Each bar is one day.
MoodEnergyChart
A line chart of your mood and energy scores over the past 30 days. Useful for spotting patterns — like "I always feel low on Mondays".
WaterNutritionChart
A combined chart showing your daily water intake and nutrition (calories/protein) side by side over the last 14 days.
VitalsCard
Yesterday's body numbers: weight, total sleep hours, deep sleep percentage, and resting heart rate. Lives inside the "More Stats" collapsed section.
StreaksCard
Your current consecutive-day streaks: training, sauna, breathwork, devotions. Also lives inside "More Stats".
CardSkeleton
A shimmering grey placeholder shown while a card is loading. Prevents the screen from jumping around as data arrives.
CardError
A small error message with a "Retry" button that appears if a card fails to fetch its data.
Hooks (the data fetchers behind the scenes)
useDashboard
Manages 3 separate data fetches: Readiness score, Vitals (yesterday's body stats), and Streaks. Each one is independent — if Streaks loads slowly, Readiness still shows.
useDashboardV2
Fetches the chart data: activity feed, mood log, water log, and food log. Also does a calculation in JavaScript — it filters today's entries from the data to build the QuickStatsRow numbers.
useGoalRings
Fetches sleep score and step count from the backend to fill in the three goal rings at the top of the page.
API calls (requests sent to the backend)
GET /api/readiness
Asks the backend to calculate today's readiness score. Returns a number 0–100 plus a written recommendation.
GET /api/summary/daily
Gets yesterday's body stats row: weight, sleep hours, deep sleep hours, and resting heart rate.
GET /api/streaks
Gets your current and longest streaks for training, sauna, breathwork, and devotions.
GET /api/activities/feed
Gets your last 14 days of activities — runs, workouts, etc. — formatted for display with dates and stats.
GET /api/mood
Gets your mood and energy log entries for the last 30 days.
GET /api/water
Gets your water log entries for the last 14 days. Each entry is a timestamp + ml amount.
GET /api/food
Gets your food log entries for the last 14 days — calories and protein per day.
Calendar Page
A month-by-month dot calendar. Coloured dots on each day represent different activity types. Tap a day to see everything that happened. Scroll up and tap "Load earlier" to fetch older months — each month loads separately and is cached so you don't re-fetch it.
Components (the visible pieces)
MonthGrid
The calendar grid itself — 7 columns for days of the week, with coloured dots inside each day cell representing your activity categories.
ToggleBar
The filter bar at the top where you switch categories on and off — e.g. show only Strength sessions, or show everything at once.
SubToggleBar
A second filter bar that appears when you've selected a single category. Lets you drill into sub-types — like filtering by specific muscle groups within Strength.
DayDetailSheet
The slide-up panel that appears when you tap a day. Shows all the activities and logs recorded for that exact date.
StatsSection
A summary panel showing statistics for the current month — total sessions per category, average duration, etc. Shown when you toggle the Stats button.
PatternsSection
Highlights patterns in your data for the current month — e.g. which days of the week you train most consistently.
MonthHeader
The sticky label (e.g. "March 2026") that stays pinned to the top as you scroll through a month's grid.
Hooks (data fetchers)
useCalendarData
Fetches one month of calendar dots at a time. When you tap "Load earlier" it fetches the previous month and prepends it to the list. Each loaded month is stored in memory so navigating back doesn't re-fetch it.
API calls
GET /api/calendar
Returns all events for a given month organised by date. Each date key has a list of dot objects — category name, colour, and any detail data for that event type.
Log Page
Two sub-tabs. "Log" tab: cards where you record today's data — water, food, strength, mood, sauna, habits. "Activity" tab: a scrollable feed of all your recent activities. Most logging uses natural language (you type it, the backend parses it).
Components — Log tab
WaterCard
Log water intake in ml. Shows today's total with a progress ring. Has quick-add buttons for common amounts (250ml, 500ml etc.).
FoodNutritionCard
Type food in plain English — "2 eggs and toast" — and the backend uses AI to figure out the calories and macros. You review them before saving.
StrengthCard
Log a strength session in plain language — describe what you lifted and the backend parses it into sets, reps, and weight for each exercise.
MoodEnergyCard
Two sliders (1–10) to log how you're feeling and your energy level right now. Saved with a timestamp.
SaunaCard
One-tap to log a sauna session. Optional duration field if you want to record how long.
HabitsCard
Checkboxes for daily habits: breathwork and devotions. Ticking them counts toward your streaks on the Dashboard.
NutritionCard
Shows today's running nutrition totals — calories, protein, carbs, fat — updated live as you log food entries.
Components — Activity tab
ActivityFeed
A scrollable list of all your recent activities — synced from Withings and manually logged. Tap any card to open full details.
ActivityDetailSheet
The slide-up panel with full details for a tapped activity — heart rate zones, duration, distance, notes.
Hooks (data fetchers)
useActivityFeed
Fetches the list of recent activities for the Activity tab. Handles loading, error, and data states.
useFoodNutrition
Handles submitting new food log entries and fetching today's running nutrition totals.
API calls
POST /api/water
Saves a new water entry. You send how many ml, the server timestamps it and saves to the database.
GET /api/water
Gets today's water entries to show the current total on the WaterCard.
POST /api/food/log
Saves a food entry with its nutrition numbers. If you typed it in natural language, the parsing happened before this call — this just saves the final result.
GET /api/food
Gets today's food entries for the running nutrition totals display.
POST /api/strength/log
Saves a strength session — which exercises, how many sets, reps, and how much weight.
POST /api/mood
Saves your mood and energy scores. Auto-timestamps the moment you tap Save.
POST /api/sauna
Saves a sauna session with optional duration.
GET /api/activities/feed
Gets the activity list for the Activity tab.
Trends Page
Long-range charts showing patterns across 30 days. When you open this page, 6 API calls fire simultaneously — sleep, heart rate, sauna, nutrition, activity, and water all load in parallel. Even if one fails, the rest still display.
Components (the visible charts)
RecoverySparklines
Small inline charts (sparklines) for your recovery metrics over time: sleep duration, sleep quality, resting heart rate, and sauna frequency. Each metric is a tiny line you can scan at a glance.
PerformanceOverlay
Overlays performance data on top of recovery metrics so you can visually spot correlations — e.g. does heavier training week lead to worse sleep?
CorrelationSummary
Plain-English insights about what seems connected in your data — e.g. "More sleep → higher readiness next day". Calculated client-side from the trend data.
NutritionTrendsChart
A weekly bar chart of your average daily protein vs the 180g target. Shows how consistent your nutrition has been week by week.
WaterTrendsChart
A 28-day bar chart of daily water intake vs the 3L target. Bars turn from ochre to green on days you hit the goal.
WorkoutVolumeChart
Charts your total training volume (sets × reps × weight) over time so you can see if your overall load is trending up or down.
ExerciseProgressSection
Tracks individual exercise progression — e.g. how your bench press best set has changed over the past 30 days. Each exercise is its own mini-chart.
Hooks (data fetchers)
useTrendsData
The big one. Fires 6 API calls simultaneously when the page loads. Collects all trend data at once. Processes the raw responses into chart-ready format — e.g. it converts individual water log entries into per-day totals.
useStrengthTrends
Fetches your strength session history — all sets, reps, and weights — to calculate volume and progression over time.
useExerciseHistory
Fetches the full history for a specific exercise (e.g. squat) to render its progression chart.
API calls (all 6 fire at the same time)
GET /api/sleep
Returns nightly sleep records — total hours and deep sleep hours. Synced from your Withings sleep tracker.
GET /api/rhr
Returns your resting heart rate readings over the time range. Lower = better recovery. Synced from Withings.
GET /api/sauna
Returns sauna session history. The hook groups them by day to show frequency over time.
GET /api/food/weekly
Returns food data already aggregated by week on the backend — average daily calories and protein per week. Much lighter than sending 30 days of raw entries.
GET /api/activity
Returns raw activity rows. The hook splits these into two buckets: runs (for the runs chart) and workouts (for strength volume).
GET /api/water
Returns individual water log entries. The hook aggregates them into per-day totals for the 28-day bar chart.
GET /api/exercises/history
Returns the history for a specific exercise — every set you've ever logged with the date, reps, and weight.
apiFetch( )
Every request passes through here · adds secret API key · handles errors
Every single API call in the entire app goes through this one function in lib/api.ts. It automatically adds a secret API key header to every request so the backend server knows the request is legitimate. If the server returns an error, the function throws an exception — which the card hook catches and uses to show the retry button.

API Endpoints

Each endpoint is a Python function that runs when the frontend calls it. GET = reads data and returns it. POST = saves new data. Hover any endpoint to see what it does.

GET/api/readiness
Calculates your readiness score by combining sleep quality, heart rate trend, and recent training load. Pure maths — doesn't save anything. Returns a score 0–100 plus a written recommendation.
GET/api/summary/daily
Reads yesterday's row from the daily_summaries table. Returns weight, total sleep, deep sleep, and resting heart rate.
GET/api/streaks
Walks backwards through your activity records day by day, counting consecutive days until it hits a gap. Does this for each streak category.
GET/api/activities/feed
Returns recent activities formatted for display — readable activity type names, date, duration, heart rate averages.
GET/api/activity
Returns raw activity rows from the database. The frontend hooks do their own filtering (runs vs workouts) on this data.
GET/api/mood
Returns mood and energy log entries from mood_logs, sorted newest-first. Accepts a "days" parameter to limit the date range.
POST/api/mood
Saves a new mood entry. Takes mood score (1–10) and energy score (1–10), adds the current timestamp, inserts into mood_logs.
GET/api/water
Returns water log entries from water_logs. Accepts "days" parameter — Dashboard asks for 14 days, Trends asks for 30.
POST/api/water
Saves a new water entry. You send ml amount, server timestamps it and inserts into water_logs.
GET/api/food
Returns food log entries from food_logs with calories and macro breakdown per day.
POST/api/food/log
Saves a food entry. If you typed natural language, AI parsing happened before this — this endpoint just saves the final numbers to food_logs.
GET/api/food/weekly
Runs a SQL GROUP BY query on food_logs to return weekly averages — average daily calories and protein per week. Much lighter than returning 30 rows.
GET/api/sleep
Returns sleep records from sleep_logs — total hours and deep sleep hours for each night.
GET/api/rhr
Returns resting heart rate readings from daily_summaries. Synced from Withings.
GET/api/sauna
Returns sauna session records. Accepts days and limit parameters for filtering.
POST/api/sauna
Saves a sauna session with date and optional duration to sauna_sessions.
GET/api/calendar
Returns a month's worth of events organised by date key. Aggregates data from activities, sleep, and food into a single response the MonthGrid can render as dots.
GET/api/exercises
Returns the list of exercises you've logged with their muscle group category (chest, back, legs, etc.).
GET/api/exercises/history
Returns the full history for a specific exercise — every logged set with date, reps, and weight.
POST/api/strength/log
Saves a strength training session. Takes exercise ID, sets, reps, and weight — stores in strength_sessions.
POST/api/sync
Triggers a Withings data sync — pulls fresh sleep, activity, heart rate, and weight data from the Withings API and saves it to the database. This is what the Sync button triggers.

Tables

Each table is like a spreadsheet — rows are individual records, columns are fields. Data either comes from Withings (synced) or from you manually logging in the app. Hover each table to see what it stores.

🗄 activities
Every activity synced from Withings — runs, workouts, walks, cycles. Each row stores the activity type, date, duration, distance, and heart rate stats. 6 years of data imported.
🗄 daily_summaries
A daily roll-up from Withings — one row per day with weight, total sleep hours, deep sleep hours, and resting heart rate. The source for VitalsCard and RHR trends.
🗄 sleep_logs
Detailed nightly sleep records synced from your Withings sleep tracker — total sleep time and deep sleep time per night.
🗄 mood_logs
Your manually logged mood entries. Each row has a timestamp, a mood score (1–10), and an energy score (1–10). Created in the app, stored here.
🗄 water_logs
Your manually logged water entries. Each row has a timestamp and how many ml you drank in that sip/glass. Multiple entries per day are summed for your daily total.
🗄 food_logs
Your manually logged food entries. Each row has a date and the nutrition breakdown: calories, protein, carbs, and fat. Typically one row per meal or food item.
🗄 sauna_sessions
Sauna sessions — either manually logged via the app or synced from Withings. Stores date, start time, and duration in minutes.
🗄 exercises
The exercise library — all the exercises you've ever logged, with their name and muscle group category (chest, back, shoulders, arms, legs, core). Referenced by strength sessions.
🗄 strength_sessions
Individual strength training sets. Each row links to an exercise and stores the number of reps and weight lifted for that set, plus a session date.
🗄 withings_tokens
The secure OAuth tokens that allow the backend to connect to your Withings account and pull data. You never see or interact with this table directly — it's purely a security detail.
External Systems
Withings API
Your Withings wearable device continuously records sleep, heart rate, steps, weight, and activities. That data sits on Withings' own cloud servers. When you tap Sync, the backend logs into the Withings API with your saved tokens and downloads everything new, then stores it in your database.
AI / NLP parsing
When you type food or exercises in natural language (like "chicken breast 200g and brown rice"), the backend sends that text to an AI language model. The AI returns structured data — calories, protein, exercise name, sets, reps — which the backend then saves to the database.
